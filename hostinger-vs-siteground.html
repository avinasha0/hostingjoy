2. Speed & Performance — 90 Days of Real Data

This is where things get interesting — and where most comparisons get it wrong. Speed depends heavily on your server location and how you measure it. We measured from 5 locations simultaneously over 90 days. Here's the complete picture.

Test LocationHostinger TTFBSiteGround TTFBWinner

The data tells a nuanced story. SiteGround is faster in the US and Europe — where its Google Cloud data centers are most densely deployed. Hostinger is faster in Asia and South America — where its 9 global data centers give it coverage SiteGround can't match.

If the majority of your audience is in the US or UK, SiteGround's Google Cloud infrastructure genuinely outperforms Hostinger in raw TTFB. If you have a global audience or your users are primarily in Asia, Hostinger is faster.

⚠️ The Speed Context That Matters Both hosts are fast enough that real users won't notice the difference. Both score 90+ on Google PageSpeed Insights with a properly optimized WordPress setup. The 200ms difference between them matters for technical SEO metrics — but for user experience, neither is slow. Don't let speed alone decide this comparison for you.

5. Control Panels — hPanel vs SiteGround Site Tools

Both hosts have moved away from cPanel in favour of their own custom control panels. This is either a feature or a frustration depending on your experience level.

🖥️
Hostinger hPanel
Clean, minimal, beginner-friendly. All the most common tasks (install WordPress, manage email, create subdomains) are surfaced prominently. The layout is familiar to anyone who has used cPanel. Slight drawback: some advanced settings are buried deeper than necessary. Best for: beginners and users running multiple sites who want a fast, streamlined experience.
⚙️
SiteGround Site Tools
More visually polished than hPanel. Excellent WordPress-specific shortcuts — staging, caching levels, and the SG Optimizer plugin are all accessible directly from the dashboard. The developer tools (Git, SSH, WP-CLI) are better organized. Best for: users who actively manage their WordPress configuration and want their host's tools deeply integrated with their workflow.

6. The Decision Framework — Who Should Choose Which

Stop trying to find a universal winner. There isn't one. Both are excellent hosts serving different priorities. Answer these questions honestly and the right choice becomes clear.

Choose Hostinger if you are:
  • Starting a blog, portfolio, or small business site
  • Running multiple websites (up to 100 on one plan)
  • Budget-conscious and watching long-term costs
  • Targeting audiences in Asia, South America, or emerging markets
  • Comfortable with chat-only support
  • Building an affiliate site or content project
  • Launching multiple client sites as a freelancer
  • On a fixed monthly budget that $17.99/mo renewal would strain
Choose SiteGround if you are:
  • Running a revenue-generating business website
  • Operating a WooCommerce store with significant daily orders
  • Non-technical and likely to need frequent support
  • Targeting primarily US and European audiences
  • A developer who values Git integration and advanced tools
  • Running a client site where downtime directly costs money
  • Managing a membership site or subscription platform
  • Someone who values phone support as a safety net
💡 The Upgrade Path Strategy Several hosting experts recommend this approach: start with Hostinger when your site is new (costs less while traffic is low, performance is more than adequate), then migrate to SiteGround or a managed host like WP Engine once your site is generating revenue that justifies the premium. Both providers offer free migration tools, so switching later costs nothing except a few hours of time.

7. Switching: How Easy Is It to Move Between Them?

One of the most common questions we get: "If I start with Hostinger, can I move to SiteGround later?" Yes — and both make it straightforward.

→
Moving to Hostinger
Hostinger offers a free migration service on Business plans and above. For other plans, their hPanel includes a Migration tool. Alternatively, use the All-in-One WP Migration plugin — it creates a full site package that installs cleanly on any host. Average migration time: 1-2 hours.
→
Moving to SiteGround
SiteGround includes free migration with every plan via their WordPress Migrator plugin. Submit a migration request to their team and they'll handle the entire process — files, database, DNS configuration. Average completion time: 2-8 hours. Our experience: seamless with zero data loss.
